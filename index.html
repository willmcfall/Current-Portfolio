About

I am an Information Management and Monitoring and Evaluation professional with 10+ years of experience working in the field of international development and humanitarian disaster response. I am currently employed as an Information Management and Geographic Information Systems Technical Officer with the American Red Cross and have previously served for 4 years as a Monitoring and Evaluation Delegate with the American Red Cross in the Philppines, 1.5 years as Monitoring and Evaluation Specialist with a USAID contractor in Kenya, and 1 year as a Volunteer Coordinator with the Foodbank of Northeast Georgia in the United States. I hold a BS degree in Agricultural and Applied Economics from the University of Illinois and a MS degree in Applied Economics from the Univeristy of Georgia. I have additionally numerous relevant short-courses including "Full-Stack Web Development" through George Washington University; "Practical Emergency Cash Transfer" and "Real-Time Evaluation" through the International Federation of the Red Cross and Red Crescent Socieites, "Project Management for Development" from , and "Monitoring and Evaluation for Humanitarian Programs" through Harvard University. I am well versed in the conceptual and technical basis of Information Management, Geographic Information Systems, Monitoring and Evaluation, Community Engagement and Accountability, Needs Assessment, Program Design, Program Management, and Web Development Graphic Design
