About

I am an Information Management and Monitoring and Evaluation professional with 8+ years of experience working in the field of international development and humanitarian disaster response. I am currently employed as an Information Management and Geographic Information Systems Technical Officer with the American Red Cross. Previously, I have served for 4 years as a Monitoring and Evaluation Delegate with the American Red Cross in the Philppines, 1.5 years as a Monitoring and Evaluation Specialist with a USAID contractor in Kenya, and 1 year as a Volunteer Coordinator with the Foodbank of Northeast Georgia in the United States. I hold a BS degree in Agricultural and Applied Economics from the University of Illinois and a MS degree in Agricultural and Applied Economics from the Univeristy of Georgia. I have additionally completed numerous data-focused short-courses including "Full-Stack Web Development" through George Washington University; "Practical Emergency Cash Transfer" and "Real-Time Evaluation" through the International Federation of the Red Cross and Red Crescent Socieites, "Project Management for Development" from , and "Monitoring and Evaluation for Humanitarian Programs" through Harvard University. With a wide range of academic and professional experiences in the fields of Information Management, Geographic Information Systems, Monitoring and Evaluation, Community Engagement and Accountability, Needs Assessment, Program Design, Program Management, Web Development, and Graphic Design, I feel that I would an asset to any organization dedicated to professionally addressing humanitarian crises. Please find my resume linked below as well as a contact form that can be used for any additional queries that you might have about my abilities. Looking forward to the possbility of speaking with you soon.
